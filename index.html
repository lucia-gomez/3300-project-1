Visualizing the percent of population vaccinated for countries of varying GDPs

This graph explores the correlation between a country's GDP and its vaccination progress. Several countries are highlighted with their flag to show a variation among countries with high and low GDPs. We wanted to see if high-GDP countries have a greater percentage of their population vaccinated and if lower-GDP countries have a smaller percentage of their population vaccinated. The lack of a clear trend is surprising, but also reassuring in that there isn't a strong disparity between which countries are able to obtain vaccines.

Visualizing the vaccination progress of selected countries from December 2020 to March 2021

As visualized in the scatterplot above, the United States, United Kindgdom, and Italy all have high GDPs, and Hungary and Israel have relatively lower GDPs, while Seychelles has the lowest GDP of the group. This linegraph expands on their vaccination progress from December 13th, 2020 to March 7th, 2021 to show surprising trends in vaccination progress over the last four months.
